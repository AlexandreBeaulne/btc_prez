Cryptocurrencies

  • New digital technology whose building blocks are modern cryptography, distributed systems and game theory
  • First time in history a digital good is scarce
  • First time in history a store of value cannot be seized without cooperation*

Cryptocurrencies

  • 1st-gen cryptocurrency: Bitcoin - started it all
  • 2nd-gen cryptocurrency: e.g. Ethereum - world's computer (smart contracts)
  • 3rd-gen cryptocurrency: e.g. ZCash, Monero - anonymous vs pseudonymous
  • Now 1000s of altcoins (a.k.a. shitcoins) exist, vast majority deserving the latter moniker

Bitcoin

  • Bitcoin Whitepaper published in 2009 under pseudonym Satoshi Nakamoto
  • Only 21,000,000 will ever exist
  • Currently ~17,062,500 have been mined
  • Last Bitcoin will be mined in ~2140

Risks & challenges

  • Hacking risk: at USD $8k / BTC, juicy target for hackers
  • Counterparty risk: this is the wild-west, nobody to turn to in case of fraud
  • Regulatory risk: Legislative grey area, and in flux
  • Crossing the fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-fiat gap difficult
